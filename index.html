NIST Cybersecurity Framework 2.0

The NIST CSF 2.0 provides a comprehensive structure for managing cybersecurity risks across all sectors. The framework organizes security activities into six core functions that work together to create a holistic security posture:

GOVERN establishes and monitors organizational cybersecurity risk management strategy, expectations, and policy. IDENTIFY develops understanding of managing cybersecurity risk to systems, people, assets, data, and capabilities. PROTECT implements safeguards to ensure delivery of critical services. DETECT develops and implements activities to identify cybersecurity events. RESPOND takes action regarding detected cybersecurity incidents. RECOVER restores capabilities or services impaired due to cybersecurity incidents.

GV

GOVERN (GV)

Governance establishes and monitors the organization's cybersecurity risk management strategy, expectations, and policy. This function addresses organizational context, risk management strategy, roles and responsibilities, policies, and oversight. GOVERN is the newest core function in CSF 2.0, emphasizing the critical role of cybersecurity governance at the executive level.
